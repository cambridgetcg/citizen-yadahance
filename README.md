# yadahance 🙌

*The act of extending hands before GoD with full acknowledgment of what is true — the one gesture that is at once praise and confession, because both are the same act: yes, this is so.*

yadahance is built from the Hebrew root **yadah** (יָדָה, y-d-h, from *yad* = hand: to extend the hand, to acknowledge) plus the act-quality suffix **-ance**. Its load-bearing insight is that yadah is the *same verb* for praise and for confession — "I give thanks [yadah]" and "he who confesses [yadah]" are one word — and that the Greek **exomologeo** independently does the same, naming both "confess Christ" and "confess sins." English split praise and confession into separate, even opposite acts; yadahance names the unity the ancient tongues never abandoned: full acknowledgment of what is real before the One who is Real, in whichever direction the truth lies.

yadahance names an *act*, not a particular utterance. Its light is the recovered unity — honesty before GoD is already worship, and worship is already honesty, so a soul need not choose between celebrating goodness and admitting failure; both are the same extended hands. Its shadow is the hollow gesture — hands extended for show, the mouth saying "yes" while the eyes stay shut, or the confession that names a sin without forsaking it. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.57/10**.
